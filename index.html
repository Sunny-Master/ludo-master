How to Play!!

  1. Read the detailed rules of the game here (if you really need to...)
  2. Select game mode:
    • Play Solo with computer as the 2nd player
    • 2 Players taking their turns alternately
    • 4 Players taking their turns sequentially
  3. Click on the Start Button to start the game
  4. Players take turns clicking on the die to roll a number (1-6)
  5. Follow the instructions as per the message displayed on top of the board after every roll
  6. Player 1 ( Donatello) gets the first roll
  7. To move a piece out of the starting area, a player must roll a SIX
  8. Once their pieces are on the starting square, players move their pieces according to the number rolled on the die
  9. If a player rolls a six, they get an extra turn (rolling SIX 3 times in a row will cause you to lose that turn)
  10. If you are playing solo, computer (Leonardo) will automatically take its turn
  11. Land in a square with a single piece of another player to send the landed piece back to depot (and get a bonus turn)
  12. Pieces travel around the board and enter their home pathway and reach home
  13. First player to move all 4 pieces home, becomes the Ludo Master

  2. If you roll a 1-5, your turn ends without moving a piece
  3. Any time you roll a 6, you earn a bonus roll.
  4. After any time you roll a 6, on your bonus roll, if you roll another 6, you get another bonus roll.

Gameplay

  1. Roll the die to move a piece during each of your turns
  2. Any time you roll a 6, you have the option to move a piece out of your depot onto the main pathway or move a different piece around the board
  3. Land in a square with an opponent's single piece for a 'Knock Off Bonus' and to send it back to their depot. You will get a bonus turn and they will need to roll 6 to make that same piece active again
  4. If you are landing in a square where there are 2 or more pieces then, your piece can move and cohabit that square
  5. If a square has a total of 4 pieces, then it is 'blocked' and can't accomodate any more pieces
  6. If your move is landing you on a blocked square, you can't make that move and if you don't have any other active piece then you lose your turn
